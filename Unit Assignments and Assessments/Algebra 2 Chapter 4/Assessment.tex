\question[12]
\begin{parts}
    \part The expression $x^5 + y^5$ can be written as the product of $x + y$ and another factor. Find that other factor.
    \vspace*{5cm}
    
    \part The expression $x^7 + y^7$ can be written as the product of $x + y$ and another factor. Find that other factor.
    \vspace*{5cm}
    
    \part Write $x^{2n+1} + y^{2n+1}$ as the product of two factors.
    \vspace*{5cm}
    
    \part Why does the factorization in the previous part fail when the powers of $x$ and $y$ are even? In other words, why can we not factor $x^4 + y^4$ or $x^6 + y^6$ using the patterns we found in the first three parts?
    \vspace*{3cm}
\end{parts}
